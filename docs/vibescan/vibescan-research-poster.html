VibeScan: A Benchmark-Driven Static Security Scanner for AI-Generated JavaScript Applications

A Data Science Poster on Experimental Design, Corpus Expansion, and Recall-Oriented Evaluation

Cybersecurity Scanner Research Team · 2026

Abstract

AI-assisted software development accelerates delivery but often introduces insecure defaults, missing guardrails, and hidden data-flow risks. We study VibeScan, a static-analysis security scanner for JavaScript and TypeScript applications with a benchmark-driven evaluation workflow.

Our experiment combines a pinned DVNA benchmark with an expanded all-check corpus designed to exercise every active VibeScan rule family plus new research classes. We report recall-oriented outcomes, corpus breadth, and proof/actionability context rather than raw issue volume alone.

Research Questions

  • Can a lightweight static-analysis scanner achieve high recall on vulnerable JavaScript applications generated or modified with AI assistance?
  • How much benchmark coverage is missed when evaluation relies only on legacy corpora such as DVNA?
  • Can we pair high recall with reproducible artifacts, proof-aware output, and CI regression gates?
  • How should scanner results be communicated so benchmark recall is not confused with raw issue count?

Hypothesis

We hypothesize that a benchmark-driven scanner tuned for JavaScript and TypeScript application structure can outperform generic baseline tools on scored benchmark recall, while also surfacing broader coverage when the benchmark corpus is expanded to match the scanner's actual rule surface.

Secondary hypothesis: proof-aware outputs and CI recall gates make high-recall behavior easier to reproduce and defend in research reporting.

System Contributions

VibeScan is a modular static-analysis package that:

  • Parses JS, JSX, TS, and TSX with semantic TypeScript support when enabled.
  • Combines rule-based detection, taint tracking, route extraction, and third-party trust-boundary analysis.
  • Exports JSON, SARIF, HTML, proof-oriented metadata, and benchmark artifacts.
  • Includes research extensions such as SSTI detection, IDOR-style direct object reference detection, and recall-first benchmark automation.

Experimental Design

Step 1 — Benchmark selectionPinned DVNA plus an expanded scanner-specific corpus with framework, crypto, SSRF, middleware, and research-class cases.
Step 2 — Ground truth encodingUse line-anchored case catalogs with expected rule identifiers for deterministic scoring.
Step 3 — Scanner executionRun VibeScan in reproducible CI-friendly modes with optional proof metadata generation.
Step 4 — Comparative analysisCompare benchmark recall against peer tools on DVNA and measure VibeScan breadth on the expanded corpus.

Datasets and Methods

  • Dataset A: DVNA benchmark with 11 adjudicated first-party vulnerability rows.
  • Dataset B: Expanded VibeScan corpus with 108 all-check rows, including 80 high-volume stress cases.
  • Combined scope: 119 scored rows across DVNA and expanded all-check coverage.
  • Metric focus: benchmark recall on anchored rows; raw issue count tracked separately to avoid misleading comparisons.
  • Quality control: deterministic case anchors, expected-rule matching, and CI regression gates.

Coverage Breadth

The expanded corpus was built to cover all active VibeScan rule families rather than only the narrower legacy DVNA slice. This includes injection-core rules, XSS/client output, crypto/secrets, SSRF, cookie/middleware checks, and new research extensions such as SSTI and IDOR.

High-volume stress rows were added to make the evaluation more statistically meaningful for poster reporting and to show that the scanner remains stable beyond one-case-per-family demonstrations.

Key Findings

  • VibeScan currently achieves top recall on the scored DVNA matrix (`11/11`).
  • The expanded VibeScan-specific corpus reaches `108/108`, demonstrating coverage beyond legacy benchmark scope.
  • The combined benchmark lane now evaluates `119` scored rows, making the experiment more representative than DVNA alone.
  • Presentation matters: recall, proof tiers, and raw issue counts must be shown as separate signals.

Interpretation, Limitations, and Future Work

The main empirical result is that VibeScan currently achieves the highest recall in the scored DVNA matrix while also sustaining full recall on a broader scanner-specific corpus. This suggests that benchmark expansion is essential: DVNA alone understates scanner breadth.

Important interpretation note: peer tools may show higher raw issue counts, but those counts are not equivalent to benchmark recall. The poster therefore separates recall, proof/actionability tiers, and raw issue volume.

Limitations: peer tools are only compared on the DVNA subset in the current readout, and the expanded corpus is intentionally VibeScan-focused rather than a universal cross-tool benchmark.

Future work: add a precision/noise lane, refresh peer tool reruns on the expanded corpus, incorporate more realistic framework benchmarks, and study remediation quality and developer adoption in end-to-end workflows.
